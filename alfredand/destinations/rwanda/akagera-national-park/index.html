Akagera National Park

Akagera National Park
Tailor-made Akagera National Park Holidays

In the land of a thousand hills, Akageras wide-open landscapes are an unexpected treasure. The low-lying, stretching-for-ever plains of this ravishingly beautiful national park, which hugs the border with Tanzania, are a glorious mosaic of habitats. Strung out along the meandering course of the Kagera River, youll find boundless lakes that are home to hippos, Nile crocodiles and a host of different waterbirds, and wetlands where swaying papyrus reeds provide a hiding place for the endangered shoebill stork. Lurking in the acacia woods or roaming the savannah grasslands are all the majestic members of the Big Five lions, leopards, bush elephants, black rhinos and Cape buffalo along with giraffes, warthogs, a dozen types of antelopes and more.

Looking at it now, its hard to believe that just two decades ago the park was overrun by cattle and on the brink of being lost forever to farmland. Its dramatic turnaround, thanks to the joint efforts of African Parks and the Rwanda Development Board, has made it a heart-warming poster child for enlightened conservation. The reintroduced lions and Eastern black rhinos are thriving, other wildlife has returned of its own accord, and the 300,000 people who live around its edge benefit from the parks various income-generating enterprises.

To experience Akageras inspiring story and landscapes for yourself, talk to our Africa gurus about making a stay here part of your dream Rwanda holiday.

  • Vicki Bramley

    Sales Manager for Alfred&

    There are spectacular views everywhere you go in Rwanda, which is one of the things that has made Vicki Bramley such a fan. Shell never forget her two-hour trek up the side of an extinct volcano to see mountain gorillas in the wild but thinks youll be just as amazed and delighted by the countrys other national parks.

  • Alissa Ruxin

    Founder and CEO of Heaven Holdings Ltd

    San Franciscan Alissa Ruxin moved to Rwanda on her honeymoon in 2005 and never left. Three years later, inspired by her foodie upbringing, she founded Heaven, a restaurant and hospitality training facility which has helped more than 1,500 Rwandans to date. Over the years, the Heaven portfolio has expanded to include hotels, restaurants, tours and experiences, a journey partly documented in husband Joshs book, A Thousand Hills to Heaven.

  • Ian Munyankindi

    Tourism and Hospitality Manager at Akagera National Park

    Rwandan-born Ian Munyankindi has been with African Parks since 2012. Prior to this, he gained his hospitality expertise in some of Rwandas best hotels and worked as a freelance guide. He joined the Akagera team as manager of Ruzizi Tented Lodge and has worked his way up to Tourism and Hospitality Manager. Ians passion for wildlife ensures that guests depart with a deep appreciation of the beauty of Akagera National Park which he jokingly refers to as his wife!

  • Protais Niyigaba

    Nyungwe Park Manager and CEO

    Born in one of the districts bordering Nyungwe, Protais Niyigaba grew up with a passion for conservation. Since completing his MSc in Biodiversity Conservation from the University of Rwanda, he has undertaken various roles in his field. He joined Nyungwe Management Company in June 2021 as Park Manager, having previously been a guide and nature interpreter there. A keen birder and botanist, he enjoys working and living in his favourite environment and being paid for doing what he loves.

  • Micheline Habineza

    Director of Operations for Question Coffee

    As Question Coffee's Director of Operations, Micheline Habineza oversees, manages and grows multiple areas of the social enterprise, including the Academy, Caf, Wholesale, Roasting and Quality Control. She joined the company as a Wholesale Account Coordinator in 2016, before taking on the position of Retail Operations Manager. Prior to that, she spent three-and-a-half years with the international in-flight catering company ASL Rwanda EPZE in Kigali as their customer liaison.

  • Marie Aimee Umugeni

    Managing director of Umutima Cooperative at Nyamirambo Womens Center

    Marie Aimee Umugeni is one of the co-founders of the Nyamirambo Womens Centre in Kigali, which was set up in 2007 to provide support to local women. It offers literacy and computer classes and teaches tailoring, enabling participants to work at the centre producing items for its Umutima Cooperative, of which Marie Aimee is managing director.
