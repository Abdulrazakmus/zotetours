Hwange National Park

Hwange National Park
Tailor-made Hwange National Park Holidays

The largest game reserve in Zimbabwe with palm-tree studded grassland and thick acacia forest, Hwange National Park, once home to Cecil the lion, is legendary among safari aficionados. For the most part, there are no rivers, but a network of pans filled with pumped water. These clever man-made life sources draw hordes of extraordinary wildlife including elegant cheetah, regal sable antelope and of course plenty of handsome lion.

While on safari here you can enjoy the startling sight of elephants in jaw dropping numbers, a given on any game drive, cooling off by the watering hole and pounding the undergrowth with their heavy feet. Look up and you may spot leopard draped in the treetops or a family of steely-eyed cheetahs scouring the plains atop towering termite mounds. Not to mention smaller creatures like the southern ground hornbill, a red-throated carnivorous bird that you can see hunting on the ground and the jolly colours of the racket-tailed roller perched in the mopane woodland.

Make the most of off-road safaris, walking explorations and night-time game drives in one of Hwange’s private concessions with the help of a safari expert.

Your journey will start with one of our UK team – someone like Becky, who's travelled extensively in Zimbabwe. They’ll shape your ideas into the trip of a lifetime. But they won't do it alone. They'll draw on the expertise of our contacts on the ground, connecting you to the people who'll make your holiday one you'll always remember – the ranger who can show you the best game viewing and fishing spots on the Zambezi, the guide who’ll introduce you to ‘moonbows’ at Victoria Falls and the locals who’ll share their dinner with you.

  • Becky Souissi

    Alfred& Tailormade Expert

    With over 20 years of experience, Becky’s passion for travel has taken her across the globe from the patterned promenades of Rio de Janeiro to the lakes of Vietnam. You’ll most likely find her in Africa where she travelled the continent trying and testing over 40 game drives. One of her favourite places in the world is the picturesque Maasai Mara for its early morning balloon rides and exceptional game viewing. It’s when among the local communities though where she feels most inspired.

  • Cloud Magondo

    Professional Guide at Nyamatusi Camp

    Cloud joined African Bush Camps after five years of experience working as a professional river guide. He has also recently achieved his Professional Guide licence, a highly regarded achievement within the safari industry. His passions are canoeing, star gazing, animal behaviour and birds while also recognising the importance of the smaller things like insects and flora. As a guide who canoes the Zambezi, he has plenty of respect for the resident hippo and crocodiles.

  • Mark Butcher

    Managing Director of Imvelo Safari Lodges

    Mark Butcher is an eco-tourism professional with a passion for linking local communities directly to wildlife conservation. In the 80s, he was a ranger for the Zimbabwe Department of National Parks and Wildlife and later, the Provincial Wildlife Officer in the Matabeleland North Province adjacent to Hwange National Park. Mark is now the Managing Director of Imvelo Safari Lodges, a selection of properties that directly benefit disenfranchised communities around Hwange.

  • Tich Tandi

    Sales and marketing manager at Wild Horizons, Alfred&’s partner in Zimbabwe

    During more than three decades in the hospitality industry, Tich Tandi has been everything from a waiter to a tours coordinator in a five-star hotel. Originally from Rusape in the eastern highlands of Zimbabwe, he now works for Wild Horizons in Victoria Falls as part of the sales and marketing team, a role he thoroughly enjoys.

  • Jane Msamba

    Deputy General Manager, Ilala Lodge Hotel

    Born in Zimbabwe, Jane began working with Ilala Lodge Hotel as a senior cashier in 1995. She has worked her way through the ranks from Junior Tours Consultant to Tours Manager and this year took up the role of Deputy General Manager of the hotel. Happily married with three children, Jane has recently become a proud grandmother. She lives in Victoria Falls with her husband and the couple is passionate about wildlife.

  • Francis Nyabadza

    Guide at Wild Horizons, Alfred&’s partner in Zimbabwe

    Francis Nyabadza grew up hearing the roar of the Victoria Falls, and familiarity has done nothing to diminish his sense of awe every time he takes guests to experience this natural wonder for themselves – something he’s been doing for Wild Horizons since 1997. When not at the Falls, his favourite place to take visitors is the village that hosts the company’s Meet the People cultural activity.
